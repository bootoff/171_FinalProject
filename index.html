Visualizing Green Energy:
A Pilot Project

Imagine driving from Boston to Perth in Western Australia.

You'd drive 11'400 miles and emit 4.7 tons of CO2.

Saving 20'397.53 metric tons of CO2 emissions is the equivalent of driving 206 times to the moon. Greenhouse gas emissions can be cut by targeting key industries that produce high levels of greenhouse gas emissions, such as water treatment plants.
This website showcases a green energy pilot initiative in Massachusetts that has resulted in cutting of over 20'000 tons of CO2 emissions.

Project Background


Why this program

Water treatment is one of the largest industries in terms of energy consumption. Wastewater and drinking water treatment facilities are struggling to meet energy usage requirements and emissions standards. Pilot studies have demonstrated that on-site renewable energy production can address both of these problems. Additional state and federal investment would allow more communities to take advantage of this enormous opportunity.

Mission

The mission of this program, Massachusetts Energy Management Pilot for Drinking water and Wastewater Treatment facilities, which was jointly launched by the Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs and Mass DEP, is to identify, develop and implement energy saving measures at municipal water and wastewater facilities resulting in reduced pollution and financial savings of at least 20%.

Progress

There are 21 facilities across Massachusetts volunteerily participated in this program, which received up to $69 million for constructing the green infrastructure projects on-site from the State Loan program, The Massachusetts’ State Revolving Fund, in 2009. And all the facilities finished installation of on-site energy generation equipment and existing equipement upgrade in 2012.
