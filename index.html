Visualizing Green Energy:
A Pilot Project

Reducing 20'397.53 metric tons of CO2 emissions is equivalent to driving 103 times to the moon and back.

Large cuts in emissions can be achieved by targeting water treatment facilities, an industry that uses large amounts of energy and produces high levels of emissions.

This website showcases a green energy pilot by the Massachusetts Energy and Environmental Affairs.
To date, the pilot has resulted in a reduction of CO2 emissions of over 20'000 metric tons. This was achieved by implementing renewable energy technologies in 20 wastewater treatment facilities in Massachusetts.

Project Background

The Problem

Water treatment facilities are among the highest energy-consuming industries. Wastewater and drinking water facilities are struggling to meet federal emission standards and energy-usage requirements, and are significant pollution contributors.

The Solution

Pilot projects have demonstrated that on-site renewable energy production in wastewater and drinking water treatment facilities can reduce the facilities' energy consumption, and lower their CO2 emissions.

The Pilot Project

A Massachusetts state-sponsored pilot project was launched in 2010 with the goal of identifying, developing, and implementing energy-saving measures at municipal water treatment facilities, so as to reduce pollution and bring about financial savings of at least 20%.

21 facilities from across Massachusetts participated in this renewable energy program.

This pilot project began in 2009, and by 2012, most plants had implemented renewable energy sources.

Key Summary Statistics per Facility

The goal of this pilot is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by getting water treatment facilities to implement renewable energy technologies on-site.

The data displayed in this graph show summary statistics for each plant since joining the pilot.

Hover over a bar to see the name of the facility, the number of years it has been a participant in the pilot and the summary data for the indicator selected.

Data Grid

The energy generated by solar panels and wind turbines at the twenty pilot facilities has translated into cost savings, as well as reduction in greenhouse emissions, in each year after installation.

Hover over a square to see the energy generated, the cost savings, or the reduction in greenhouse gas emission for each facility for each fiscal year. The horizontal and vertical bars at the margins show the totals for each facility across all years and the totals for each year across all participating facilities.

A few large facilities dominate the program and highlight the impact of these installations.

Data Timeline

Starting in 2012, the year of implementation for most facilities, savings incurred from use of renewables rose and electricity usage generally decreased or remained steady for each facility starting in that year.

Mouse over the plot for more detailed, year-by-year data. Use the selection box to see different parameters recorded over the course of the project.
