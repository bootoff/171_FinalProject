Visualizing Green Energy:
A Pilot Project

Reducing 20'397.53 metric tons of CO2 emissions is equivalent to driving 103 times to the moon and back.

Large cuts in emissions can be achieved by targeting water treatment facilities, an industry that uses large amounts of energy and produces high levels of emissions.

This website showcases a green energy pilot by the Massachusetts Energy and Environmental Affairs.
To date, the pilot has resulted in a reduction of CO2 emissions of over 20'000 metric tons. This was achieved by implementing renewable energy technologies in 20 wastewater treatment facilities in Massachusetts.

Project Background


The Problem

Water treatment facilities are among the highest energy-consuming industries. Wastewater and drinking water facilities are struggling to meet federal emission standards and energy-usage requirements, and are significant pollution contributors.

The Solution

Pilot projects have demonstrated that on-site renewable energy production in wastewater and drinking water treatment facilities can reduce the facilities' energy consumption, and lower their CO2 emissions.

The Pilot Project

A Massachusetts state-sponsored pilot project was launched in 2010 with the goal of identifying, developing, and implementing energy-saving measures at municipal water treatment facilities, so as to reduce pollution and bring about financial savings of at least 20%.

Summary Statistics

The bar charts show the summary statistics for each treatment plant since joining the pilot project.

Data sources

The data is collected from three main sources:

  • MassEnergyInsight Database (MA DOER)
  • Facility Response To Request
  • Powerdash, Solrenview, Facilities and State Vendor

With the participation from both public and private sectors, facilities joined this project have achieved a higher energy efficiency and contribute largely to the climate change.

Data Grid

Greenhouse gas emission reduction

The result of carbon dioxide emission reduction is notable. For all the participants, a total amount of 39.8 million lbs of CO2 was reduced since the project started in 2007. Waste Water Pilot contributed 49.1% of all the emission reduction.

Energy Generation

Total energy generation on site among all the participants has reached $8.67 million. waste Water Pilot facilities contributed to 55.5% of the total generation. And Falmouth Wastewater Treatment Facility has produced the most renewable energy through wind power, which has a total amount of $2.2million over the past few years.

Data Timeline

Electricity Usage

Many of the facilities have experienced a increasing electricity savings in the past years. Reasons behind that are different, most of them have applied the fund in upgrading their equipement to improve energy efficiency. Others have reallocated resources to reduce their energy usage. Yet, because of the new equipments, and weather conditions in the winter, in some areas facilities have experienced an increase usage of electricity, which is reflected on the graph as downward sloping lines.

Usage vs. Cost Scatter Plot

Highlight

Two facilities have the total usage savings over 1 million dollars: Greater Lawrence Sanitary District and Ashland Drinking Water Facility. The large cost saving is based on many different factors, for example, the size of the facility, the energy generated on site, etc.
